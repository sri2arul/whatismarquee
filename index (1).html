What in the world is "<marquee>"?

A MARQUEE REFERS TO A LARGE OUTDOOR TENT OR A PROMINENT ROOF-LIKE SIGN ABOVE AN ENTRANCE. However, that is not what your'e here for.
The term "<marquee>" in the context of HTML is a legacy element in html used to create moving or scrolling text horozontally or vertically across a web page.
FUN FACT: "<Marquee>" was originally a turf war between Microsoft and Netscape. They forced the removal of "<marquee>" to get rid of the annoying "<blink>" tag on the other side.
